Mosquito Mode

Purpose

A playful movement practice that awakens the body, resets classroom energy,
and builds group rhythm.

How it begins

The teacher says quietly:

“Mosquito Mode.”

Then:

"They're hungry today..."

Students imagine invisible mosquitoes trying to land anywhere on their bodies.

The goal:

Don't let them land.

Students begin swatting, twisting, ducking, hopping, and laughing.

Within seconds the room becomes a field of movement.

Pulse Ladder (Student Leadership)

A student leader sets the mosquito level:

None around  
A few  
Some  
Wow…  
Oh dear…  
AHHHHH!!!

The class responds with movement intensity.

Each student eventually gets a turn leading.

Leadership Discovery

Leaders must model the energy they call.

If someone shouts “AHHH!” but barely moves, the room mirrors the mismatch.
Usually with laughter.

Students quickly learn that leadership is not declaring energy,
but embodying it.

Resonance

After several rounds the room may begin to synchronize.

Energy moves through the group like a pulse or a wave.

When this shared rhythm appears, the game often ends naturally.

Alternate Ending

On chaotic days the teacher can simply say:

“Circle.”

The movement itself prepares the group for listening.

For Teachers

Mosquito Mode works for adults too.
It can be a joyful warm-up in teacher workshops or meetings.
