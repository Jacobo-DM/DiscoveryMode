A field practice

Pocket Walk

Do not hunt for the line. Learn the field.

Pocket Walk is a simple practice for noticing subtle shifts in the world around you: warmth, coolness, dampness, airflow, mist, light, hush, and change.

Walk slowly.

Try your palms outward, downward, and upward.

When something changes, stop.

Do not rush to explain it.

Did you feel that?

The sequence

Feel → Compare → Read the Field → Measure → Wonder Again
1 · Feel

Notice the change before naming it too quickly.

Warmer, cooler, wetter, drier, softer, sharper, quieter, brighter, stranger.

2 · Compare

Ask others what they noticed.

Difference is not failure. It is part of the inquiry.

3 · Read the field

Look around before reaching for tools.

Shade, slope, wind, bark, moss, dampness, insects, soil, water, exposure.

4 · Measure

If useful, use tools to sharpen the question.

Thermometer, hygrometer, notebook, pH strip.

5 · Wonder again

Ask what happened here, what remains unclear, and what changed when you stopped to notice.

Palm orientations

Outward

What is moving across me?

Air, temperature, drift, dampness, fluctuation.

Downward

What is rising from below?

Stored heat, coolness, ground moisture, terrain presence.

Upward

What is arriving from above?

Sun, mist, shade, brightness, falling cool, canopy openings.

Group form

Anyone in the group may call a stop. When someone notices a change, pause together and ask:

Did you feel that?

Then move through the sequence: Feel → Compare → Read the Field → Measure → Wonder Again

If you want to test further, return to the spot and ask:

Did the pocket stay with us, or did it pass through?

Notes for practice

Some pockets stay in place.

Some pass through.

Some are carried by the body.

Some are mixed.

Not every pocket resolves into a clear answer.

Some become findings.

Some remain questions.

Both matter.

A quiet reminder: the body is also part of the field.

The world is made of pockets, not partitions.
