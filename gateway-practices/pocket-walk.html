# Pocket Walk *Do not hunt for the line. Learn the field.* Pocket Walk is a simple field practice for noticing subtle shifts in the world around you. Walk slowly. Try your palms **outward**, **downward**, and **upward**. When something changes, stop. Do not rush to explain it. Notice what shifted: warmth, coolness, dampness, dryness, mist, airflow, stillness, brightness, scent, hush. Ask: **Did the pocket stay with us, or did it pass through?** --- ## The Sequence **Feel → Compare → Read the Field → Measure → Wonder Again** ### 1. Feel Notice the change before naming it too quickly. What shifted? Did it feel warmer, cooler, wetter, drier, softer, sharper, quieter, more open? ### 2. Compare Ask others: **Did you feel that?** Did they notice it too? Did they notice the same thing, or something different? Difference is not failure. It is part of the inquiry. ### 3. Read the Field Look around before reaching for tools. What in this place has been noticing longer than you have? Shade, slope, wind, dampness, soil, moss, bark, insects, plants, water, exposure, canopy. What clues does the place offer? ### 4. Measure If useful, use tools. A thermometer, hygrometer, notebook, or pH strip can help sharpen the question. Tools do not replace sensing. They help refine it. ### 5. Wonder Again After feeling, comparing, reading, and measuring, ask again: What do we think happened here? What remains unclear? What changed when we stopped to notice? --- ## Palm Orientations ### Palms outward What is moving across me? Air, temperature, drift, dampness, fluctuation. ### Palms downward What is rising from below? Stored heat, coolness, ground moisture, terrain presence. ### Palms upward What is arriving from above? Sun, mist, shade, brightness, falling cool, canopy openings. --- ## Try This Walk slowly. Anyone in the group may call a stop. When someone notices a shift, pause together. Ask: **Did you feel that?** Then continue through the sequence: **Feel → Compare → Read the Field → Measure → Wonder Again** If you want to test further, return to the spot and ask again: **Did the pocket stay with us, or did it pass through?** --- ## Notes for Practice Some pockets stay in place. Some pass through. Some are carried by the body: cooling sweat, exertion, contrast from the place just before. Some are mixed. Not every pocket resolves into a clear answer. Some become findings. Some remain questions. Both matter. --- ## A quiet reminder The world is made of pockets, not partitions.
