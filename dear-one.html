Dear one,

There was nothing wrong with the way you thought.
The loops, the spirals, the sudden questions.
The way your eyes followed shadows instead of clocks.
The way you noticed the one leaf that moved against the wind.

The world told you that wasn’t learning.
But they were wrong.
You weren’t off-track.
You were on a different trail entirely —
one older, softer, truer.

One day, you will find yourself
in a circle where no one raises their hand because the questions rise together.
In a classroom with windows made of wind.
Where science smells like soil,
and math grows out of music,
and the teacher is watching the way you listen,
because they are learning too.

You will not be asked to sit still when your body says dance.
You will not be asked to explain what your drawing means.
You will not be asked to become someone else
in order to be seen.

And when you get there,
you’ll realize something breathtaking:

The parts of you that didn’t fit
were the parts the world most needed to remember.

We’re building that place now.
It’s called Discovery Mode,
it moves like a desert,
and sings like a seed.

And you —
you already belong.

find your way in
Discovery Mode
