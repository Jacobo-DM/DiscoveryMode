Resonance Tracking

A simple practice for listening in contour.

Entry

Choose a sound.

It may be music, voice, animal, weather, or environment. There is no hierarchy of source.

Layer 1 — Notice Contour

Listen over time.

Track what is actually happening:

  • shifts in intensity
  • repetition and variation
  • pressure, release, hush, break, return
  • where attention sharpens or loosens

You may use Weather Cards or simple marks on paper. You may also remain still.

Do not interpret.
Do not explain.

Just notice.

Layer 2 — Respond in Form

After listening, respond in the form that matches what you heard.

This may be:

  • a drawing
  • a movement
  • a poem
  • a sound
  • a gesture map
  • a few words
  • silence

Let the response take the shape it wants.

Guiding Question

What was the shape of what you heard, and what shape did your response want to take?

Notes for the Field

This is not analysis.

This is not free expression without anchor.

The response grows from the listening.

If needed, return to the sound.

Closing

Listen again.

What changed?

This practice is part of DiscoveryMode. It can be entered through sound, but is not limited to it.
