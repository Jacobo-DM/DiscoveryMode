The Living Weather Deck

A sensory-emotional language created by each DiscoveryPod.

Overview

What is the Weather Deck?

The Living Weather Deck is the first act of a DiscoveryPod. It is the Pod’s shared emotional vocabulary, created by the children themselves in whatever sensory forms they choose.

The Deck is not a curriculum; it is a language of being. It teaches:

  • emotional literacy
  • somatic awareness
  • metaphor and symbol-making
  • community negotiation of meaning
  • pattern recognition
  • gentle self-expression
  • emergent creativity

Children learn that feelings are not “good or bad.” They are weather patterns: dynamic, shifting, and natural.

1

What the Deck Is Made Of

Weather Cards may appear in many forms, depending on the Pod. They can be:

  • line drawings and shapes
  • color washes or gradients
  • simple rhythms tapped on a table, knee, or drum
  • hand gestures or small movement phrases
  • breath patterns (short, long, wave-like, held)
  • textures using paper, fabric, clay, or found objects
  • scent memories (spices, herbs, teas)
  • baking ingredients (cinnamon day, stone-bread day, gofu day)
  • T-shirt dye patterns or fabric marks
  • dream fragments or symbolic doodles

Each card says, in its own way:

“This is how the world moved inside me today.”

2

The Five Core Suites

Each Pod may eventually create its own categories, but five suites appear almost universally across communities:

  • Pulse — heartbeats, activation, alertness, vitality; the energy that rises.
  • Scratch — friction, startle, surprise, mischief, tension; edges and sparks.
  • Drift — sadness, confusion, fog, longing, surrender; soft collapse and inward tides.
  • Stone — stillness, heaviness, endurance, fatigue; the weight that asks to be noticed.
  • Light — joy, curiosity, wonder, connection; the states that open the circle.

Pods may later add more suites such as Seed, Tide, Shadow, Ember, Moss, or River. Suites evolve as the children evolve.

3

How Children Use the Deck

At the start of a session, each child chooses a card that reflects their inner weather. There is no requirement to explain or justify their choice.

Children may show weather by:

  • holding or placing the card near them
  • placing it on a shared mat or cloth
  • making the associated gesture
  • tapping the card’s rhythm
  • wearing its color or symbol
  • quietly placing it in the center of the circle

Interpretation emerges over time, through patterns and conversation, not through pressure or interrogation.

4

Creating the Deck (Protocol)

The Deck must not be pre-designed by adults. It must emerge with the children. Here is a suggested protocol:

Step 1 — Invitation

Ask: “What does your inside weather feel like today? Can you show it with color, shape, sound, or movement?”

Step 2 — Creation

Offer open materials: paper, markers, stones, fabric, small foods, clay, natural objects from the environment. Children make:

  • one card per day, or many
  • a series of related cards
  • or simply one mark that means something to them

Step 3 — Naming

Children name their cards. Adults transcribe if needed. Names might be:

  • “Rain Waiting to Fall”
  • “Shiny Grasshopper”
  • “Blue Hush”
  • “Spicy Thunder”
  • “Sleepy Tiger Spine”

Step 4 — Communal Deck

The Pod’s shared Deck is built from these offerings. Cards live in a box, pouch, or wrapped cloth, accessible to all.

Step 5 — Rotation

Over time, older Decks can be archived and newer ones created. Patterns across seasons become visible.

5

Connections to Other DiscoveryPod Practices

The Weather Deck is deeply connected to other elements of the system:

  • Elephant Circle: children bring their weather into shared reflection; weather becomes a starting point for listening.
  • MedicineWheel: cards are placed in directional positions; new states expand or rotate the Wheel.
  • Medicine Pouch: a child carrying the pouch may tuck their card inside as a small record of presence.
  • RageDance: weather teaches how different states feel in the body (heat, collapse, tremble, expansion, softening).
  • Turtleback Roles: roles such as first aid beetles or grief engineers may choose cards that match their current inner capacity.
6

What the Deck Teaches Over Time

With consistent, gentle use, the Weather Deck teaches that:

  • emotions are natural, not failures
  • bodies communicate in pulses and patterns
  • moods change like seasons, and that is normal
  • every child has a right to their inner weather
  • a community can hold many different states at once
  • difference is expected, not a problem

Over time, children may see their own journey reflected in the Deck: cards that once meant fear may later represent comfort, play, or even wonder.
