The Living Weather Deck

A sensory-emotional language created by each DiscoveryPod.

Overview

What is the Weather Deck?

The Living Weather Deck is the first act of a DiscoveryPod. It is the Pod’s shared emotional vocabulary, created by the children themselves in whatever sensory forms they choose.

The Deck is not a curriculum; it is a language of being. It teaches:

  • emotional literacy
  • somatic awareness
  • metaphor and symbol-making
  • community negotiation of meaning
  • pattern recognition
  • gentle self-expression
  • emergent creativity

Children learn that feelings are not “good or bad.” They are weather patterns: dynamic, shifting, and natural.

1

What the Deck Is Made Of

Weather Cards may appear in many forms, depending on the Pod. They can be:

  • line drawings and shapes
  • color washes or gradients
  • simple rhythms tapped on a table, knee, or drum
  • hand gestures or small movement phrases
  • breath patterns (short, long, wave-like, held)
  • textures using paper, fabric, clay, or found objects
  • scent memories (spices, herbs, teas)
  • baking ingredients (cinnamon day, stone-bread day, gofu day)
  • T-shirt dye patterns or fabric marks
  • dream fragments or symbolic doodles

Each card says, in its own way:

“This is how the world moved inside me today.”

2

The Five Core Suites

  • Pulse — activation, alertness, vitality
  • Scratch — friction, surprise, sparks
  • Drift — fog, sadness, inward tide
  • Stone — heaviness, endurance, stillness
  • Light — joy, curiosity, wonder
3

How Children Use the Deck

At the start of a session, each child chooses a card that reflects their inner weather.

  • holding or placing the card near them
  • placing it on a shared mat
  • making the associated gesture
  • tapping the card’s rhythm
  • wearing its color or symbol
  • placing it quietly in the center

Interpretation emerges through time, not interrogation.

4

Creating the Deck (Protocol)

Invitation

“What does your inside weather feel like today?”

Creation

Children make cards using open materials.

Naming

Children name their weather.

Communal Deck

The cards become the Pod’s shared weather language.

5

Connections to Other DiscoveryPod Practices

  • Elephant Circle: weather becomes a starting point for listening.
  • WeatherWheel: cards may be placed directionally to explore patterns.
  • DiscoveryPouch: a child carrying the pouch may tuck their card inside as a small record of presence.
  • RageDance: weather teaches how different states move through the body.
  • Turtleback Roles: roles may choose cards that match their current inner capacity.
6

What the Deck Teaches Over Time

  • emotions are natural
  • bodies communicate in patterns
  • moods change like seasons
  • every child has a right to their weather
  • a community can hold many states at once

Over time, children may see their own journey reflected in the Deck.
